Before you make
an offer

The part that happens before the fun part. Get clear, solve your numbers, and get to an accepted offer without falling for a home you cannot actually buy.

Here is what a lot of agents do: they get excited and start touring right away. I do it differently, because I want to protect you from the one thing that actually hurts, falling in love with a home that was never possible. So before we tour a single house, we solve your numbers. Then the search becomes the easy, fun part.

Step One

Know what you actually want.

Before anything else, get honest about what is driving this and what you truly need. When we talk, I will ask you to tell me everything first, because the more you share, the better I can help. Sit with these on your own to start.

Why now? What has you wanting to buy at this point, and what are you leaving, renting, outgrowing, relocating?
Needs versus wants. What does the home have to have, and what would just be nice to have?
Location. Which areas, and what matters most, commute, schools, being near people, land?
Tell me everything. Beds, baths, yard, style, dealbreakers, timeline. Write it down while it is fresh, and we recap together so nothing gets missed.
Step Two

Solve your numbers before you tour.

This is the step most people want to skip, and it is the one that protects you most. A pre-approval is proof you can qualify. If you do not qualify yet, we would much rather find that out now and fix it than after you have fallen for a home. There are really only three numbers that matter, and the list price is not one of them.

Step Four

We start looking together.

The good news: you do not have to wait for the pre-approval to finish before we start. I begin the search right away, so the two run side by side and you feel real momentum. Then we tour with your numbers set. When we walk a home, I look past the staging, at the roof, the systems, the layout, and the things a fresh coat of paint hides. That is the part I am especially good at catching.

One honest warning: do not fall for the first one before your financing is locked. Excitement is normal. Just keep the numbers in the driver's seat until the pre-approval letter is in hand.

Step Five

Write one that gets accepted.

When you find it, a strong offer is about more than the highest number. Price gets attention, but terms often win the deal.

Your pre-approval letter. This is why we did lending first. A clean, strong letter tells the seller you are real.
Earnest money. Your good-faith deposit. A solid amount signals you are serious.
Contingencies and dates. How you structure due diligence, financing, and settlement timing can matter as much as price.
A clean, simple offer. Sellers love certainty. Fewer complications often beats a slightly higher, messier bid.

The goal is to stand out without overpaying. A well-structured offer at a fair price often beats a higher one that looks shaky. This is exactly where having someone in your corner earns its keep.
